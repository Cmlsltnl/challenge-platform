What does "belonging" mean to you?

What does "belonging" mean to others?

We interviewed students, parents, educators and researchers to find out what belonging means to them. Here’s what they told us:

Danyelle Durall, DC parent & educator

Belonging is: When my children and students have the same access & aspirations as their peers, whether black, white, or Hispanic

Jonathan Rivers, DC school social worker

Belonging is: Absolutely essential for students to feel that they are able to grow and trust.

Mai S., San Francisco student

Belonging is: People accepting me as a black muslim girl and acknowledging me as a regular human being

Jada P., DC student

Belonging is: A place where you are supposed to be and where you are needed

Paige Hoffman, DC Public Schools staff

Belonging is: When the weight of the world and all the expectations people have of you just come off

William Gardener, DC uncle

Belonging is: Being a productive part of my community where we all work together to educate our children

Our First Challenge

Over the next few months, Sevenzo is bringing together students and educators from across the country to find what works to create a sense of belonging and spread those bright spots to as many places as possible.
Be part of a community of students and educators who care deeply about fostering belonging. We’re talking to individuals from across the country → connecting conversations → platform. We welcome students and classroom teachers as well as principals, support staff, and district administrators to join our community, because it takes all of you to create a sense of belonging in schools. Our challenges platform, launching later this fall, is designed to be a space for meaningful conversation, a place to translate ideas into action.
Find the bright spots in communities like yours. Around the country, students are starting the school year feeling like they are back in a place where they belong. We think that we should highlight and celebrate what’s already working in classrooms, schools, and communities, and we want to hear what’s happening in your community.
Spread what works to more places. We don’t believe there are cookie-cutter solutions in education, but we don’t have to start from scratch, either! We look for the core ingredients and process steps to understand what makes each bright spot work, creating flexible implementation recipes. Along the way, we’ll share what our community is learning about how the recipes evolve to light up new bright spots.

About Sevenzo

Sevenzo is a new social impact nonprofit dedicated to building community to spread what works so that all learners can reach their full potential. Our name means the communal effort that makes change possible. With your help, we can ensure that all students feel a sense of belonging in their schools and communities so that they can learn and thrive.
